What is AI?


AI (artificial intelligence) is the mimicking of human intelligence which is processed by a computer/ robot to preform common tasks that is associated with human intelligence like the ability to reason, discover meaning and learn from past experiences (Copeland, 2023). There are two types of AI weak and strong. Weak AI is a system designed and trained for a specific task but cannot work without human interactions e.g. (Alexa or computer chess). Strong AI is a system that can solve tasks and problems without human interaction e.g.(self-driving cars).

What can it do:


  1. Learn- AI systems have the ability to learn from data allowing the system to ingest large amounts of information

  2. Reason – AI can analyse information and make logical decisions based on logic and rules given to it. Which can range from simple to complex problems.

  3. Remember- AI systems have the ability to retain information so that when new problems with the same type of solution it can pick it up very quickly

  4. It helps to get critical pieces of information across to people in a simpler manner

What is information systems in business?


This the organizing, collection, processing and distribution of information. It’s a combination of data, processing and people working to support operations, management and decision-making in an organisation. Through the use of computer systems and other technologies, information systems for businesses give organisations the information they need to run themselves successfully and efficiently. (Miss, 2023) (Copeland, 2023)

Components of information system:

It can be very difficult to sometimes make sense of the diagrams and graphs. It can sometimes the information can be misinterpreted or the data can be designed wrong and the diagram could be biased or hard to interoperate.


  1. Hardware – physical devices e.g. computers, servers and input devices it’s the physical components that can be seen and touched.

  2. Software – this the applications and programs these cannot be physically held e.g. operating systems

  3. Processes – these are the methods on how the data is collected and processed and used

  4. Users – this refers to the people that interact with the system e.g. employees, managers and customers

Roles of Information Systems in Business:


  1. Operational support – helps automate tasks, manage business operations and improves efficiency e.g. transaction processing systems and inventory management.

  2. Decision support – this provides information to the managers to make sure the best decision is made it is done using analysing tools like pattern recognition to help with decisions.

  3. Strategic Advantages – this the basically giving of competitive advantages e.g. new business models, improving products and services and reducing costs.

  4. Data Management – this the sorting, managing and organising data in a structured way e.g. databases

The benefits of AI in information systems in businesses:


The use of AI in information systems in business will be benefit for businesses because of its ability to handle large amounts of data and give accurate feedback makes AI the perfect tool to use. For instance, AI can handle repetitive tasks and if give the right data will rarely have errors even if it is complex strategic work. Ai also has the ability to recognize patterns meaning it will greatly assist in help managers make better decisions allowing for faster decisions on being made. As well as being advantageous it is cost effective for the business its like employing a person who never gets tired and knows everything.

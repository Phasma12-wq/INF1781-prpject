What is Data Visualization?


The use of images to represent information it is meant to make understanding information easier. by taking information and putting it in a visual format such as graphs and diagrams. Putting extensive amounts of information from words to a simple visual format.

Uses of Data Visualization

People’s eyes are easily capture bright and colourful patterns and images. By putting information in a visual format it grabs a person’s attention and interest making it easier for information to be absorbed.


  1. Date scheduling

  2. Recognizing patterns

  3. Predictability: by seeing the patterns you can determine the outcomes of the future e.g. Risk assessment we can determine how great a risk is just by simply compiling the information in to a visual aspect and see where the links to the risks are from.

  4. It helps to get critical pieces of information across to people in a simpler manner

Advantages of Data Visualization

People’s eyes are easily capture bright and colourful patterns and images. By putting information in a visual format it grabs a person’s attention and interest making it easier for information to be absorbed.


  1. Simplifies information because the information is in pictures instead of words

  2. Helps compile information in a simpler manner

  3. Helps to discover patterns easier which in turn helps with predictability

  4. It makes memorising information more easily

Disadvantages of Data Visualization

It can be very difficult to sometimes make sense of the diagrams and graphs. It can sometimes the information can be misinterpreted or the data can be designed wrong and the diagram could be biased or hard to interoperate.


  1. In accurate information could be given or mostly biased

  2. The diagram could be too confusing making harder instead of easier to interoperate
